# Ethics, Policy, License, and Disclaimer  

---

## Overview  
This document outlines the ethical considerations, intended use, limitations, risk mitigation strategies, license, and disclaimer for the Traffic Sign Classifier system.

The project focuses on the responsible development and evaluation of artificial intelligence models using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) and reinforcement learning techniques. The system is intended strictly for academic and research purposes.

---

## Intended Use  
The Traffic Sign Classifier is designed to:
- Demonstrate the application of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) in image classification  
- Support academic learning and experimentation in computer vision  
- Serve as a prototype model for traffic sign recognition  

This system is **not intended for deployment in real-world or safety-critical environments**, such as autonomous driving systems, without extensive further development and validation.

---

## Dataset Scope and Limitations  
The dataset used in this study includes a limited set of traffic sign categories:
- Traffic lights    
- Speed limit signs  
- Stop signs  

### Limitations:
- The model represents only a narrow subset of real-world traffic conditions  
- Performance results are limited to the dataset used  
- The system may not generalize well to different environments, lighting conditions, or unseen traffic signs  

---

## Ethical Risks  

- **Misclassification Risk**  
  Incorrect predictions may lead to unsafe outcomes if used improperly  

- **Overgeneralization**  
  Users may assume the model is suitable for real-world deployment  

- **Safety Concerns**  
  Use in real-world traffic systems without validation may pose risks  


## Risk Mitigation Strategies  

- Clearly labeled as **academic and experimental only**  
- Documentation highlights all limitations and constraints  
- No claims of real-world readiness  
- Users are warned against safety-critical use  

---

## Responsible Use  

Users are expected to:
- Use the system only for educational and research purposes  
- Avoid real-world deployment without proper validation  
- Acknowledge dataset and model limitations
